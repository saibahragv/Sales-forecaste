# 📊 AI Sales Intelligence Platform: Detailed Guide

This document provides a comprehensive breakdown of the data, the machine learning model, and every single feature in the user interface.

---

## 💾 The Data: Where it Comes From & How it's Used

### 1. Data Source
The platform uses a time-series dataset located in `backend/data/train.csv`. 
- **Type**: Historical retail sales data.
- **Granularity**: Daily sales by Store and Item.
- **Key Columns**:
    - `date`: The calendar date of the sales record.
    - `store`: A unique identifier for the retail location (Store ID).
    - `item`: A unique identifier for the product (SKU/Item ID).
    - `sales`: The total quantity sold on that day.

### 2. Feature Engineering (The "Brain")
Before the data is fed into the AI, the system automatically creates "intelligence features" to help the model understand patterns:
- **Time Features**: Day of week, month, year, and whether it's a weekend.
- **Lag Features**: Previous sales (e.g., "What did we sell yesterday? Last week?").
- **Rolling Stats**: Moving averages and volatility (e.g., "What is the average sales over the last 30 days?").
- **Cyclical Encoding**: Mathematical representations of seasons (Spring, Summer, etc.).

### 3. The Model
- **Algorithm**: **LightGBM** (Light Gradient Boosting Machine).
- **Why?**: It is world-class for tabular time-series data because it is incredibly fast and can handle complex, non-linear relationships that simple Excel models miss.
- **Inference**: The model is "pre-trained." When you click a button, the backend performs a "live inference" to predict the future based on the latest history.

## 🖥️ UI Feature Guide (Every Button Explained)

The interface is divided into several specialized "Intelligence Labs." All of them use the **Global Filter Bar** at the top.

### 🛠️ The Global Filter Bar (Top of Screen)
This bar controls the "Scope" of what you are looking at.
- **Store / Item Selectors**: Choose which specific location and product you want to analyze.
- **Anchor Date**: The "As-Of" date. The AI uses data *up to* this date and starts its forecast from the next day. This is useful for "backtesting" (seeing how the AI would have performed in the past).
- **Aggregation (Daily/Weekly/Monthly)**: Group the data to see long-term trends or granular day-to-day fluctuations.
- **Horizon Slider**: Control how many days into the future the AI should look (up to 90 days).
- **Normalize (Checkbox)**: Converts raw sales into an index (Base 100). Useful for comparing a high-volume store with a low-volume store on the same scale.
- **Confidence Band (Checkbox)**: Shows a shaded "Uncertainty Zone" around the forecast. If the area is wide, the AI is less certain; if it's narrow, the prediction is highly stable.

### 1️⃣ Executive Overview (`/overview`)
**Purpose**: A high-level dashboard for decision-makers.
- **Growth KPI**: The percentage increase/decrease in predicted sales compared to history.
- **Volatility**: Measures how "bumpy" the demand is. High volatility means you need more safety stock.
- **Stability**: A score of how consistent the patterns are.
- **Risk Score**: A combined metric telling you how likely you are to have stock-outs or overstock.
- **Plain-Language Summaries**: Automated text generated by the AI to explain the metrics in simple English.

### 2️⃣ Forecast Explorer (`/forecast`)
**Purpose**: Visualizing the future line.
- **Main Chart**: Shows the historical sales (solid line) and the AI's prediction (dashed line).
- **Decomposition Panel**: This breaks the sales down into three parts:
    - **Trend**: The long-term direction (Is it generally going up or down?).
    - **Seasonal**: Recurring patterns (e.g., "We always sell more on Saturdays").
    - **Residual**: The "Noise" or random fluctuations that can't be explained by patterns.

### 3️⃣ Scenario Simulation Lab (`/scenario`)
**Purpose**: The "What-If" simulator. **This is a unique feature.** You can move sliders to see how the forecast changes:
- **Macro Demand Shock**: Simulates an external event (like a recession or a sudden market boom).
- **Promotion Intensity**: What happens if we run a massive marketing campaign?
- **Price Elasticity**: Adjusts how sensitive customers are to price changes.
- **Growth Slope**: Simulates a steady increase in market share.
- **Competitive Pressure**: See the impact of a new competitor opening nearby.
- **Demand Cap**: Sets a maximum limit (e.g., "We only have capacity for 500 units").

### 4️⃣ Risk & Stability (`/risk`)
**Purpose**: Stress-testing your supply chain.
- **Risk Indicator**: Highlights periods of high uncertainty.
- **Stability Metrics**: Helps planners decide where to focus their attention.

### 5️⃣ Explainability (`/explainability`)
**Purpose**: Removing the "Black Box." It uses **SHAP** values to show *why* the AI made a choice.
- **Global Importance**: Shows which factors generally matter most for this product (e.g., "Price matters more than Seasonality").
- **Local Explanation**: Click a specific day in the future to see exactly why that specific number was predicted.

### 6️⃣ AI Assistant (`/assistant`)
**Purpose**: Natural Language interaction.
- **Chat Interface**: You can ask questions like "Why is the risk high for Store 1?" or "Explain the forecast for next week." The system uses its internal data to give you a detailed answer.

## 🛠️ Technology Stack: The Libraries We Use

The platform is built on a modern AI stack. Each library has a specific role in training, testing, and serving the sales forecasts.

### 🧠 Machine Learning & Data Science
- **LightGBM (`lightgbm`)**: 
    - **Role**: The core AI engine. 
    - **Usage**: Used to train the gradient-boosted decision trees that predict future sales. It is chosen for its high performance and ability to handle large datasets with categorical features (like Store and Item IDs).
- **SHAP (`shap`)**: 
    - **Role**: Explainable AI (XAI).
    - **Usage**: Calculates the contribution of each feature to a specific forecast. It translates "Black Box" model outputs into the "Why" behind the numbers.
- **Scikit-Learn (`scikit-learn`)**: 
    - **Role**: Data preprocessing and utilities.
    - **Usage**: Used for label encoding, scaling, and splitting data into training and testing sets.
- **NumPy (`numpy`) & SciPy (`scipy`)**: 
    - **Role**: Numerical computation.
    - **Usage**: Powers the mathematical engines behind scenario simulations and risk calculations.
- **Pandas (`pandas`)**: 
    - **Role**: Data manipulation.
    - **Usage**: The "Excel" of the backend. It handles all data loading (`train.csv`), filtering, and feature engineering.

### 🌐 Backend & API
- **FastAPI**: 
    - **Role**: Web framework.
    - **Usage**: High-performance, asynchronous framework used to serve the AI predictions to the frontend.
- **Uvicorn**: 
    - **Role**: ASGI Server.
    - **Usage**: The lightning-fast server that runs the FastAPI application.
- **Pydantic**: 
    - **Role**: Data validation.
    - **Usage**: Ensures that the data coming in (requests) and going out (forecasts) is structured and error-free.
- **Joblib**: 
    - **Role**: Model persistence.
    - **Usage**: Used to save the trained LightGBM model to a `.pkl` file and load it instantly at startup.

### 🎨 Frontend & AI Assistant
- **React (Vite + TypeScript)**: 
    - **Role**: User Interface.
    - **Usage**: Provides the interactive dashboard, charts, and dark-themed executive UI.
- **Sentence-Transformers**: 
    - **Role**: Natural Language Processing (NLP).
    - **Usage**: Powers the **AI Assistant** chat, helping it understand your questions about the sales data.
